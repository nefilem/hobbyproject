Longshaw Estate and Burbage Edge, Derbyshire

Two great places to go if you want to get out of Sheffield and have a walk, Burbage Edge is popular with climbers throughout the year along with sheep who do their best to avoid people. Burbage Edge has been the site of quarrying over the years and remnants of mill wheels can still be seen in some locations and Burbage Brook meanders through Burbage Valley with Burbage Edge to the east and Carl Wark and Higger Tor to the west. This area of Derbyshire is particularly rich in archaeological remains with cairns, stone circles, signs of quarrying and more interestingly Carl Wark itself which is an ancient Iron Age hill fort. Longshaw Estate itself is part of the National Trust so if you're a member you get free parking on the estate itself, parking is for a time free over at Fox House pub but spaces are a commodity. Other car parks of which there are several around the area require a small fee for parking.
